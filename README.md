# Undercover

A social word‑deduction party game for your group. One phone each, one host, no app store.

**▶ Play now: https://iamyvj.github.io/localundercover/**

Everyone gets a secret word — but not everyone gets the *same* one. Describe your word without giving it away, sniff out the impostors, and vote them off before they blend in and take over.

---

## The idea

- **Civilians** all share the same secret word.
- **Undercover** players get a *similar but different* word — they have to blend in.
- **Mr. White** (optional) gets no word at all, and has to bluff from what everyone else says.

## Each round

1. **Describe** — in turn, everyone says one word or short phrase that hints at their word. Never say the word itself.
2. **Vote** — everyone votes to eliminate one player. Whoever's out reveals their role.

## Winning

- **Civilians** win when every undercover and Mr. White has been eliminated.
- **Undercover** side wins the moment they equal or outnumber the civilians.
- **Mr. White** counts toward that — and if voted out, gets one guess at the civilians' word. Nail it exactly and Mr. White steals the win.

The host chooses how vote ties are settled: a **runoff** between the tied players, a **runoff then random** pick, or simply **no elimination** that round.

---

## How to play

1. One person enters their name and taps **Create game** (local Wi‑Fi) or **Host on server** (players join from anywhere), then shares the 4‑letter room code.
2. Everyone else enters that code and their name to join.
3. The host picks a word category and role counts, then starts.
4. Hold your card to peek at your secret word — it only ever shows on your own device.

Supports **4–20 players**, with no accounts and nothing to install — add it to your home screen as an app if you like.

## Local or online

Two ways to host, picked when you start a game:

- **Create game** — peer‑to‑peer over your local Wi‑Fi. No server, works fully offline; everyone just needs to be on the same network.
- **Host on server** — the game runs on a shared always‑on server, so players can join from **anywhere**, on any network. This option appears when the server is reachable.

A small badge shows whether the current game is running **on the server** or over local **Wi‑Fi**, and the home screen shows whether the server is reachable right now.
